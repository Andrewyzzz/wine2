\noindent
This anonymized supplement provides the deployment summary statistics used
in Section~7 of the submission, so that the reported figures can be
verified without de-anonymizing the authors. The underlying per-auction
records and the reproduction scripts are included in the accompanying
archive. We reiterate the scope stated in the main text: these are
\textbf{observable deployment diagnostics, not estimates of the structural
complementarity factor $g$}. The trace records winner and runner-up
solution scores and order-level notionals, but not the full vector of
per-leg standalone valuations required to compute $V(S)/\sum_j V(\{j\})$;
the worst-case results of the submission hold for any $g$.

\section*{S2. Visible coverage / spread distribution}

For each auction, let $\mathrm{Score}_1$ be the winning solution's score
and $\mathrm{Score}^{\mathrm{other}}_2$ the best positive score submitted
by a different solver. Define the observable
\[
\widehat\alpha^{\mathrm{obs}}
=\frac{\mathrm{Score}^{\mathrm{other}}_2}{\mathrm{Score}_1}\in(0,1],
\qquad
\widehat G^{\mathrm{obs}}=1/\widehat\alpha^{\mathrm{obs}}\ge1 .
\]
This is an \emph{auction-level} visible coverage; it is a proxy for, and
weaker than, the per-leg coverage parameter of the submission's
coverage law, which would require per-leg standalone benchmarks.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{@{}lr@{}}
\toprule
Event & Share of auctions \\
\midrule
Visible spread $\widehat G^{\mathrm{obs}}>2$ (all orders) & $25.3\%$ \\
Visible spread $\widehat G^{\mathrm{obs}}>4$ (all orders) & $20.2\%$ \\
Visible spread $\widehat G^{\mathrm{obs}}>2$ (small-notional orders) & $63.1\%$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Tail of the visible-spread distribution. A non-trivial spread is
the common case rather than a corner, especially for small orders.}
\end{table}

local window with no inheritance or smoothing), we replay a single
low-quote injection per cell-window and record whether it would move the
